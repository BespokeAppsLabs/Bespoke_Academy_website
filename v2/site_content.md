## 9. FAQ SECTION

### Parent Questions

**Q: Is my child the right age for this program?**
A: This program is specifically designed for students in grades 8-11 (ages 13-17). The curriculum, equipment, and teaching methods are all tailored for teenage learners. If your child is slightly outside this range, please contact us to discuss their readiness and individual needs.

**Q: Does my child need any prior programming or technical experience?**
A: Absolutely not! This program is designed for complete beginners. We start with basic computer confidence and build skills progressively. The only requirement is interest and willingness to learn and try new things.

**Q: How much does the program actually cost with all equipment included?**
A: The monthly fee is R1500 for 10 months (total R15,000). This includes over R5000 in professional equipment that your child keeps, all software and learning materials, weekly instruction, and ongoing parent communication. There are no hidden fees.

**Q: What exactly will my child build and keep?**
A: Over 40 weeks, your child will build: a complete computer workstation with Raspberry Pi, electronic projects with circuits and sensors, multiple robots with increasing complexity, AI-powered devices with cameras and microphones, and a final capstone project demonstrating all learned skills. All of this equipment becomes their personal technology toolkit.

**Q: How do you ensure safety with electronics and tools?**
A: Safety is our top priority. All equipment is age-appropriate with built-in safety features, students receive comprehensive safety training, instructors are first-aid certified, and all activities are professionally supervised. We use low-voltage electronics and maintain strict safety protocols.

**Q: What if my child misses a session?**
A: We provide make-up sessions and detailed summaries of missed content. Students also receive all equipment and materials to practice at home. Our instructors work individually with students to help them catch up and stay on track.

**Q: How will I know my child is actually learning and making progress?**
A: You'll receive weekly email updates with photos and videos of projects, quarterly showcase events where students demonstrate their work, access to a parent portal with progress tracking, and a complete portfolio of your child's achievements by program end.

**Q: Can my child continue learning and building after the program ends?**
A: Absolutely! Since students keep all their equipment, they'll have a complete home technology lab for continued innovation. We also provide resources for advanced projects and connection to the broader maker community.

**Q: What makes this program different from other STEM or coding classes?**
A: This program combines four unique elements: comprehensive 40-week progression (not just one-off classes), integration of AI concepts with hands-on robotics, equipment ownership model (students keep everything), and focus on both technical skills and essential life capabilities like problem-solving and perseverance.

**Q: How do you handle students with different learning speeds and interests?**
A: Our 10:1 student ratio allows for personalized attention. Projects can be adapted to different skill levels and interests. We encourage students to customize projects based on their passions while ensuring core learning objectives are met.

### Program Logistics

**Q: What is the time commitment required?**
A: Friday sessions are 2.5 hours, plus 1-2 hours per week of optional home practice. Showcase events occur quarterly. Total weekly investment is 3-4 hours including the Friday session.

**Q: Are there any additional costs beyond the monthly fee?**
A: Only potentially R200-500 for specialized final project components if students choose premium parts. All basic equipment, software, and materials are included. We provide complete transparency about any potential additional costs upfront.

**Q: What happens if we need to miss sessions due to travel or family circumstances?**
A: We offer flexible pause options and make-up sessions. Students can continue learning with their equipment at home while traveling. Our goal is to accommodate family needs while ensuring learning progress.

**Q: How do you handle different experience levels among students?**
A: We embrace diverse experience levels! Peer learning is encouraged, students with more experience can take on leadership roles, and projects can be scaled for complexity. All students start at the same point and progress together.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What kind of computer or equipment do we need at home?**
A: Nothing is required - we provide everything students need. However, having a computer with internet access at home is beneficial for practice and exploration. We can provide recommendations if families want to set up a home workspace.

**Q: Is this program in-person or online?**
A: [Specify based on actual delivery model - could be in-person, online, or hybrid]. Our approach ensures hands-on learning regardless of delivery method, with equipment provided for home use if needed.

**Q: How do you incorporate AI into a program for teenagers?**
A: We teach AI as a tool for creativity and problem-solving. Students learn to use AI assistants for learning support, understand AI basics and ethics, verify AI-generated content critically, and integrate AI concepts into their robotics projects.

### College and Career Preparation

**Q: How will this program help with college applications?**
A: Students develop a comprehensive portfolio including: project documentation and videos, code repositories and technical writing, innovation journals and reflection essays, and evidence of long-term commitment and skill development.

**Q: What career paths does this program prepare students for?**
A: This program builds foundation skills for any technology-related career: software development, engineering, data science, AI/ML, robotics, entrepreneurship, and digital innovation. More importantly, it teaches students how to learn and adapt to new technologies.

**Q: Do you provide college counseling or career guidance?**
A: While not a primary focus, we provide guidance on STEM education pathways, connect students with mentors in technology fields, and help them position their portfolios for college applications and internships.
