# Bespoke Academy AI Robotics Curriculum Framework

This framework is specifically designed for **Grades 8-11 students (ages 13-17)** with **no prior knowledge** of computers, programming, AI, or robotics. The curriculum transforms complete beginners into confident creators through a 40-week hands-on learning journey, meeting once weekly for 2+ hours on Fridays.

## Program Philosophy

### **"Zero to Hero" Approach**
- **Start from Absolute Zero**: No assumptions about any prior technical knowledge
- **Interactive Learning**: Learn by doing, not by memorizing or taking tests
- **AI-Assisted Development**: Use AI tools as learning assistants throughout
- **Tangible Outcomes**: Every week results in something that works and can be demonstrated
- **Confidence Building**: Focus on success, curiosity, and creative problem-solving

### **Target Audience**
- **Grade Levels**: 8-11 (ages 13-17)
- **Prerequisites**: None - truly designed for complete beginners
- **Class Size**: 10-15 students optimal for individual attention
- **Session Format**: 2+ hours every Friday
- **Learning Style**: Hands-on, project-based, collaborative

## Program Structure

### **Complete 40-Week Journey**
The curriculum is structured as a single comprehensive track that progresses through four distinct phases:

### **Phase 1: Digital Foundations (Weeks 1-8)**
*Transforming computer anxiety into confidence*
- Computer literacy and file organization
- Visual programming with Scratch
- Introduction to Python programming
- First physical computing with Raspberry Pi and LEDs

### **Phase 2: Electronics & Robotics Basics (Weeks 9-16)**
*Mastering the physical world of electronics and movement*
- Understanding electricity and circuits
- Working with sensors (light, temperature, distance)
- Motor control and basic movement
- First robot assembly and remote control

### **Phase 3: AI Concepts & Tools (Weeks 17-28)**
*Understanding and using AI as a creative assistant*
- AI fundamentals and ethics
- Mastering AI tools like ChatGPT/Claude
- Computer vision and voice recognition
- Training custom AI models

### **Phase 4: Integrated AI-Robotics Projects (Weeks 29-40)**
*Creating unique AI-assisted robotic prototypes*
- Project planning with AI assistance
- Advanced sensor integration
- Building and programming complete prototypes
- Final showcase and demonstration

## Learning Objectives

### **Technical Skills (Beginner to Advanced)**
- **Digital Literacy**: Confident computer use and file organization
- **Programming Fundamentals**: Visual programming (Scratch) → Python programming
- **Electronics & Circuits**: Basic electricity → Complex sensor integration
- **Robotics**: Simple circuits → Autonomous AI-assisted robots
- **AI Literacy**: Understanding AI concepts → Training custom models
- **Integration**: Combining hardware, software, and AI into complete projects

### **Essential Life Skills**
- **Problem-Solving**: Systematic troubleshooting and logical thinking
- **Creativity**: Designing and building unique projects
- **Perseverance**: Working through challenges and debugging
- **Collaboration**: Helping peers and learning from others
- **Communication**: Explaining technical concepts and demonstrating projects
- **Confidence**: Transforming "I can't" into "I can figure this out"

## Teaching Methodology

### **"Learn by Doing" Approach**
1. **Immediate Success**: Every session results in a working project
   - 30-minute introduction and demonstration
   - 60-minute guided hands-on building
   - 30-minute testing, demonstration, and reflection

2. **AI-Assisted Learning**
   - AI tools as learning assistants and tutors
   - Prompt engineering and effective AI collaboration
   - Critical thinking about AI responses and limitations

3. **Progressive Confidence Building**
   - Start with guaranteed successes
   - Gradually increase complexity
   - Celebrate every achievement and learning moment

### **No Grades - Growth Focus**
**What We Assess Instead:**
- **Project Completion**: Is the weekly project working?
- **Understanding**: Can the student explain what they built?
- **Problem-Solving**: How do they approach challenges?
- **Collaboration**: Do they help and learn from peers?
- **Portfolio Development**: Documentation of learning journey
- **Final Demonstration**: Working AI-assisted robotic prototype

### **Learning Environment**
- **Safe to Fail**: Mistakes are learning opportunities
- **Collaborative**: Students help each other succeed
- **Curiosity-Driven**: Questions encouraged and explored
- **Inclusive**: Multiple ways to succeed and demonstrate learning
