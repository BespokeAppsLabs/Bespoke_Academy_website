# Safety Guidelines
*Comprehensive safety procedures for the AI Robotics Curriculum*

## Overview

Safety is the foundation of all learning in the AI Robotics program. These guidelines ensure that students can explore electronics, programming, and robotics in a secure, supervised environment. All activities are designed with age-appropriate safety measures for Grades 8-11 students.

## Safety Philosophy

- **Safety First**: No activity compromises safety for learning objectives
- **Age-Appropriate**: All equipment and activities are suitable for teenagers
- **Supervised Learning**: Adult supervision is required for all hands-on activities
- **Progressive Responsibility**: Safety skills build alongside technical skills
- **Risk Awareness**: Students learn to identify and mitigate risks independently

---

## General Laboratory Safety

### **Workspace Safety Rules**

#### **Personal Safety**
- **Eye Protection**: Wear safety glasses when working with tools or moving parts
- **Appropriate Clothing**: No loose clothing, ties, or jewelry that could get caught
- **Closed-Toe Shoes**: Required at all times in the workspace
- **Tied-Back Hair**: Long hair must be secured when working with equipment
- **No Food/Drink**: Keep food and beverages away from electronics and computers

#### **Workspace Organization**
- **Clean Workspace**: Keep work areas clear of clutter and unnecessary items
- **Proper Lighting**: Ensure adequate lighting for all activities
- **Clear Pathways**: Maintain clear walkways between workstations
- **Tool Storage**: Return tools to proper storage after use
- **Waste Disposal**: Dispose of electronic waste properly and separately

#### **Behavior Expectations**
- **No Running**: Walk, don't run, in the workspace
- **Focused Attention**: Pay attention to tasks, especially when using tools
- **Ask Questions**: Never hesitate to ask if something is unclear or seems unsafe
- **Report Issues**: Immediately report any safety concerns or damaged equipment
- **Respect Equipment**: Use tools and components as intended

### **Emergency Procedures**

#### **In Case of Emergency**

2. **Alert Instructor**: Notify the instructor immediately

## Electrical Safety

### **Low Voltage Safety**
All curriculum activities use low voltage (5V-12V DC), but basic electrical safety still applies.

#### **Basic Electrical Safety Rules**
- **Dry Hands**: Never touch electrical components with wet hands
- **Power Off**: Disconnect power before modifying circuits
- **One Hand Rule**: When possible, use only one hand to touch live circuits
- **No Metal Tools**: Never use metal tools on live circuits
- **Check Connections**: Verify all connections before applying power

#### **Battery Safety**
- **Proper Polarity**: Always connect batteries with correct polarity (+/-)
- **Battery Types**: Use only approved battery types (AA, AAA, 9V, LiPo for specific projects)
- **Charging**: Charge batteries only with approved chargers and under supervision
- **Storage**: Store batteries in cool, dry places away from metal objects
- **Disposal**: Dispose of batteries according to local regulations

#### **Component Handling**
- **Capacitors**: Handle charged capacitors carefully (though rare in this curriculum)
- **LEDs**: Connect LEDs with appropriate resistors to prevent burnout
- **Motors**: Be careful of moving parts and pinch points
- **Sensors**: Handle sensors gently to avoid damage

### **Circuit Safety**

#### **Circuit Building Rules**
- **Double-Check Connections**: Verify all connections before applying power
- **No Short Circuits**: Ensure positive and negative terminals don't touch
- **Current Limits**: Use appropriate resistors to limit current flow
- **Heat Management**: Monitor components for excessive heat
- **Secure Connections**: Ensure all wires are securely connected

#### **Troubleshooting Safety**
- **Power Off**: Disconnect power before modifying circuits
- **Visual Inspection**: Look for obvious problems before testing
- **Step-by-Step**: Test circuits incrementally
- **Ask for Help**: Don't hesitate to ask if something seems wrong

## Tool Safety

### **Hand Tools**

#### **Screwdrivers**
- **Right Size**: Use the correct size screwdriver for each screw
- **Firm Grip**: Hold screwdrivers firmly but not too tight
- **Direction**: Apply force in the direction of rotation, not sideways
- **Storage**: Store screwdrivers in holders or toolboxes

#### **Pliers and Cutters**
- **Cut Away**: Always cut wire away from yourself and others
- **Proper Use**: Use pliers for gripping, not hammering
- **Sharp Edges**: Be aware of sharp cut wire ends
- **Insulated Tools**: Use insulated tools when working with electricity

#### **Wire Strippers**
- **Correct Setting**: Use the appropriate gauge setting for wire size
- **Controlled Pressure**: Apply steady, controlled pressure
- **Clean Cuts**: Make clean cuts without damaging wire strands
- **Safety Check**: Verify wire is completely stripped before use

### **Measuring Tools**

#### **Multimeters**
- **Correct Setting**: Always select the correct measurement type and range
- **Probe Care**: Handle probes carefully, don't touch metal parts during measurement
- **High Voltage**: Never measure high voltage (not part of this curriculum)
- **Probe Placement**: Place probes correctly for accurate measurements

#### **Rulers and Calipers**
- **Careful Use**: Use measuring tools carefully to avoid injury
- **Sharp Edges**: Be aware of sharp edges on metal rulers
- **Proper Storage**: Store measuring tools to prevent damage

## Equipment Safety

### **Computers and Electronics**

#### **Computer Safety**
- **Power Cables**: Secure power cables to prevent tripping hazards
- **Ventilation**: Ensure computers have adequate ventilation
- **Liquid Spills**: Keep all liquids away from computers
- **Proper Shutdown**: Turn off computers properly when finished

#### **Raspberry Pi and Microcontrollers**
- **Static Electricity**: Be aware of static discharge risks
- **GPIO Pins**: Handle GPIO pins carefully to avoid damage
- **Power Requirements**: Use correct power supplies and voltage
- **Heat Management**: Monitor for overheating during intensive use

### **Sensors and Input Devices**

#### **Camera Safety**
- **Privacy**: Respect privacy when using cameras
- **Lens Care**: Handle camera lenses carefully to avoid scratches
- **Proper Mounting**: Secure cameras firmly to prevent falls

#### **Microphones**
- **Volume Levels**: Use appropriate volume levels to protect hearing
- **Proper Positioning**: Position microphones correctly for best results
- **Handling**: Handle microphones gently to prevent damage

### **Output Devices**

#### **LEDs and Displays**
- **Brightness**: Avoid looking directly at very bright LEDs
- **Heat**: Monitor LEDs for excessive heat during extended use
- **Power Limits**: Don't exceed recommended power ratings

#### **Motors and Actuators**
- **Moving Parts**: Keep fingers and loose clothing away from moving parts
- **Pinch Points**: Be aware of pinch points in mechanisms
- **Secure Mounting**: Ensure motors are securely mounted before operation
- **Load Limits**: Don't exceed motor load specifications

## Chemical and Material Safety

### **Soldering (Optional Advanced Activities)**

*Note: Basic curriculum avoids soldering, but these guidelines are provided for advanced electives*

#### **Soldering Safety**
- **Ventilation**: Solder only in well-ventilated areas
- **Eye Protection**: Wear safety glasses when soldering
- **Heat Safety**: Be aware of hot soldering iron tips
- **Solder Fumes**: Avoid breathing solder fumes
- **Lead-Free Solder**: Use lead-free solder when possible

#### **Soldering Equipment**
- **Iron Stand**: Always place soldering iron in stand when not in use
- **Damp Sponge**: Use damp sponge for cleaning iron tip
- **First Aid**: Know burn first aid procedures
- **Fire Safety**: Keep fire extinguisher nearby

### **Adhesives and Materials**

#### **Glues and Epoxies**
- **Ventilation**: Use adhesives in well-ventilated areas
- **Skin Contact**: Avoid skin contact with adhesives
- **Eye Protection**: Wear safety glasses when using adhesives
- **Proper Storage**: Store adhesives according to manufacturer instructions

#### **Cutting Materials**
- **Sharp Tools**: Use appropriate cutting tools for each material
- **Cutting Direction**: Cut away from yourself and others
- **Safety Glasses**: Wear eye protection when cutting materials
- **Dust Control**: Control dust when cutting certain materials
